About Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli is an Indian cricketer and the former captain of the Indian national team. He is regarded as one of the best batsmen of his generation and has numerous records to his name.

Kohli was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. He developed an interest in cricket at a young age and started his training at the West Delhi Cricket Academy when he was just nine years old.

Career

Early Career

Kohli made his debut for India in August 2008 against Sri Lanka. His early career was marked by a rapid rise through the ranks of Indian cricket, establishing himself as a reliable middle-order batsman.

International Success

Over the years, Kohli has been a crucial part of the Indian team in all formats of the game. He has been known for his aggressive batting style, incredible consistency, and the ability to chase down challenging targets.

Captaincy

In 2017, Kohli was appointed as the captain of the Indian cricket team in all formats. Under his leadership, India achieved several historic victories, including winning Test series in Australia and achieving the number one ranking in Test cricket.

Achievements

  • ICC ODI Player of the Year: 2012, 2017, 2018
  • ICC Test Player of the Year: 2018
  • Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna: 2018
  • Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy: 2017, 2018
  • Fastest batsman to 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, 11,000, and 12,000 runs in ODI cricket
  • Most centuries in run-chases in ODI cricket
  • Most double centuries by an Indian in Test cricket

Personal Life

Virat Kohli married Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma in December 2017. The couple is known for their philanthropic work and often engages in various charitable activities. They welcomed their daughter, Vamika, in January 2021.

Philanthropy

Kohli is actively involved in various charitable endeavors. He established the Virat Kohli Foundation, which aims to support underprivileged children and promote sports in India. The foundation regularly organizes events and collaborates with other organizations to provide education and healthcare to those in need.
